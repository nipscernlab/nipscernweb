Year 1: The Toolkit - Propositional & First-Order Logic

0%

Q1: The Foundations of Reasoning

Smith, Peter - An Introduction to Formal Logic
Full Read
The best modern starting point. Work through all exercises systematically. This is your foundation.

Q2: Classical Logic & First Principles

Plato - Euthyphro, Meno, & Theaetetus
Full Read
Witness the Socratic method. The search for definitions and the problem of knowledge are the beginnings of logic.
Aristotle - Organon (Selections: Categories, On Interpretation, Prior Analytics I)
Full Read of Selections
The original formal system: categories, propositions, and syllogistic logic. Understand its structure and genius.

Q3: The Modern Revolution

Frege, Gottlob - Begriffsschrift (with commentary)
1879 | Original Paper
The conceptual leap of quantification that began modern logic. Read a guide alongside it.
Russell, Bertrand - On Denoting & Mathematical Logic as Based on the Theory of Types
1905 & 1908 | Original Papers
Understand Russell's solution to paradoxes and the motivation for type theory, which led to Principia Mathematica.

Q4: Consolidation & History

Kneale & Kneale - The Development of Logic
Reference Text
This is your historical reference for the entire 6-year journey. As you learn a new topic (e.g., modal logic), read the corresponding chapter here to understand its history.
Review and Practice
Self-directed
Revisit Smith's exercises. Solve skipped problems. Teach concepts aloud to solidify them.

Year 2: The Limits - Incompleteness & Computability

0%

Q1-Q2: Gödel's Incompleteness Theorems

Smith, Peter - An Introduction to Gödel's Theorems
Full Read (Dedicated Half-Year)
The best guide available. Give this text the time it deserves. Master Gödel numbering, the diagonal lemma, and the proofs of both theorems.
Hofstadter, Douglas - Gödel, Escher, Bach
1979 | Parallel Read
Builds intuition and wonder about strange loops and self-reference. Not a formal text, but essential for context.
Gödel, Kurt - On Formally Undecidable Propositions...
1931 | Original Paper
After finishing Smith's book, read the original paper with the help of a guide (like van Heijenoort's).

Q3: Turing and the Halting Problem

Petzold, Charles - The Annotated Turing
Full Read
A guided, line-by-line reading of Turing's seminal paper. More accessible than diving straight into the original. Master Turing Machines.

Q4: The Church-Turing Thesis

Sipser, Michael - Introduction to the Theory of Computation
Full Read of Part One: Automata and Computability
A modern, clear text that formalizes the Church-Turing thesis and explores other undecidable problems.

Year 3: The Universes - Set Theory & Model Theory

0%

Q1: Axiomatic Set Theory

Halmos, Paul - Naive Set Theory
1960 | Full Read
An intuitive but rigorous introduction to the language of modern mathematics: ordinals, cardinals, and paradoxes.
Enderton, Herbert - Elements of Set Theory
Full Read
The formal development of Zermelo-Fraenkel with Choice (ZFC). Do the exercises.

Q2: Independence & Forcing

Smullyan & Fitting - Set Theory and the Continuum Problem
Full Read
An accessible introduction to the ideas of forcing and the independence of the Continuum Hypothesis.

Q3: Foundations of Model Theory

Marker, David - Model Theory: An Introduction
Full Read of Chapters 1-4
The study of mathematical structures using logic. Marker is the modern gold standard. Master Compactness, Löwenheim-Skolem, and Quantifier Elimination.

Q4: Advanced Model Theory

Marker, David - Model Theory: An Introduction
Full Read of Chapters 5-8
Continue with Marker to be introduced to types, stability, and other core concepts of 20th-century model theory.

Year 4: Alternatives - Non-Classical & Philosophical Logics

0%

Q1: Modal Logic

Priest, Graham - An Introduction to Non-Classical Logic
Full Read
A brilliant survey of the non-classical landscape. Start with his chapters on modal logics to master Kripke semantics.
Kripke, Saul - Naming and Necessity
Full Read
A foundational text in the philosophy of language that shows the philosophical power of modal thinking.

Q2: Applications of Modal Logic

Fagin, Halpern, et al. - Reasoning About Knowledge
Full Read
The application of modal logic to knowledge (Epistemic Logic) and belief. Crucial for computer science and AI.
Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy: "Deontic Logic" & "Temporal Logic"
Article Study
Use these high-quality articles to get a rigorous introduction to the logic of obligation/permission and the logic of time.

Q3: Intuitionistic & Substructural Logics

Return to Priest's "An Introduction to Non-Classical Logic"
Focused Reading
Now, work through the chapters on Intuitionistic Logic (the logic of constructive proof).
Restall, Greg - An Introduction to Substructural Logics
Full Read
Logics that question basic structural rules, like weakening or contraction. Includes relevance and linear logic.

Q4: Paraconsistent & Quantum Logics

Priest, Graham - In Contradiction
Full Read
An introduction to Paraconsistent Logic and Dialetheism: the idea that some contradictions can be true.
SEP: "Quantum Logic and Probability Theory" & "Inductive Logic"
Article Study
Use the SEP to get a rigorous introduction to Quantum Logic (which denies distributivity) and the vast field of Inductive Logic (reasoning under uncertainty).

Year 5: The Structure - Proof Theory & Categorical Logic

0%

Q1: Structural Proof Theory

Troelstra & Schwichtenberg - Basic Proof Theory
Full Read
From truth (model theory) to proof (proof theory). Master natural deduction and sequent calculus.
Gentzen, Gerhard - Investigations into Logical Deduction
1935 | Original Paper
Understand the significance of the cut-elimination theorem (Hauptsatz).

Q2: The Curry-Howard Correspondence

Sørensen & Urzyczyn - Lectures on the Curry-Howard Isomorphism
Full Read
A detailed text on the profound discovery that proofs are programs, and propositions are types. Unifies logic and computation.

Q3: Category Theory

Awodey, Steve - Category Theory
Full Read
The language of modern mathematics: categories, functors, natural transformations, limits, and adjunctions.

Q4: Categorical Logic & Topoi

Goldblatt, Robert - Topoi: The Categorial Analysis of Logic
Full Read
A topos as a generalized universe of sets, where intuitionistic logic is the internal language. A challenging but rewarding read.

Q1: How We Connect

Full Read

Q2: Interpreting Cultures

Geertz, Clifford - The Interpretation of Cultures
Full Read
A foundational text of modern anthropology. Understand the method of "thick description" and see cultures as systems of symbols and meaning.

Q3: Language & Thought

Pinker, Steven - The Language Instinct
Full Read
An accessible and powerful introduction to the science of language, from universal grammar to how children learn to speak.

Q4: Power & Ritual

Confucius - The Analects
Full Read (Slingerland translation)
The foundation of much of East Asian thought on social order, ritual (Li), humaneness (Ren), and the cultivation of character.

Year 6: Synthesis, Philosophy & Original Work

"This final year is not about acquiring new knowledge, but about weaving the threads of logic and universal understanding into a coherent worldview. You move from student to architect. You will ask your own questions and begin the lifelong project of answering them, building a mind that is not merely full, but functional, integrated, and wise."

Q1: Philosophy of Logic, Science, and Mathematics

Haack, Susan - Philosophy of Logics
Full Read
Now that you know many logics, you can ask: Is there one true logic, or many? What is the relationship between logic and reality?
Kuhn, Thomas - The Structure of Scientific Revolutions
Full Read
Integrate your knowledge of history and science. How does scientific knowledge actually progress? Understand paradigm shifts and normal science.

Q2: The Frontiers and Deep Dives

Homotopy Type Theory: Univalent Foundations
The Frontier of Logic
Read the preface and introduction to grasp the current frontier: treating proofs as spaces, unifying logic, topology, and computer science.
Choose Your Frontier
Self-directed Research
Deep dive into one advanced area that fascinates you. Read recent survey papers on arXiv or the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy.

Q3: Teaching & Integration

The Feynman Technique Project
Test of Understanding
Choose three difficult topics (e.g., Forcing, Gödel's Second Theorem, Categorical Logic) and write a clear, simple explanation for an intelligent undergraduate. This is the ultimate test of your own understanding.
Cross-Disciplinary Essays
Synthesis Essays
Write three short essays connecting different domains. How does Gödel's incompleteness relate to themes in existential literature? How can model theory be applied to linguistics? What is the relationship between thermodynamics and economic theory?

Q4: Original Contribution

Write Your Synthesis
Personal Magnum Opus
Produce a 50-100 page work that articulates your integrated worldview. It could be a philosophical treatise, a logical analysis of a cultural phenomenon, or a framework for a principled life. This is your first original contribution.
Design Your Next Decade
A Living Document
You are now an architect. What will you build? Formulate the guiding questions and projects for the next phase of your life, based on the solid foundation you have constructed.
